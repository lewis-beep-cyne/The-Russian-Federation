Russia's Coat of Arms

The Russian coat of arms features a golden double-headed eagle on a red shield, symbolizing sovereignty and continuity from the Byzantine tradition. It was restored in 1993 after the Soviet era, and remains the official state emblem today.

Comparison: Imperial vs. Modern Coat of Arms

Feature Imperial Russia (pre-1917) Modern Russia (post-1993)
Eagle Double-headed, black Double-headed, gold
Crowns Imperial crowns Three crowns linked by ribbon
Central Shield Saint George, plus smaller shields of regions Only Saint George
Symbolism Empire's dominion Federation's sovereignty
